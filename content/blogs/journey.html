Welcome to the beginning of our journey. Our mission is to replace synthetic, petroleum-based plastics with sustainable packaging grown from nature itself. In this post, we take a deep dive into the technology behind our MycoComposite™ material, walking you through the entire growth process and explaining the qualities of the final product.

The Growing Process: Step-by-Step

Unlike traditional manufacturing which requires high heat, petroleum, and chemical synthesis, we partner with biology. Here is exactly how we grow our packaging:

Step 01

Substrate Sourcing & Sterilization

We start with regional agricultural byproducts, mainly hemp hurd and agricultural waste. This material is cleaned and sterilized to ensure a clean growing environment, free from competing organisms.

Step 02

Inoculation & Pre-Colonization

The sterilized agricultural waste is mixed with nutrients and inoculated with our premium mycelium strain. For the first few days, the mycelium is left to colonize the loose substrate in bulk containers, forming a dense white network of micro-fibers.

Step 03

Molding & Growth

We break up the pre-colonized substrate and pack it into custom-engineered 3D thermoformed molds. Over a period of 4 to 7 days, the mycelium continues to grow, serving as a self-assembling natural adhesive that binds the loose agricultural fibers together into the exact shape of the mold.

Step 04

Deactivation & Drying

Once the growth is complete, the packaging is demolded and put into drying ovens. The low heat dehydrates the material and deactivates the mycelium, stopping all future growth. This ensures the product is completely stable, clean, and safe for international shipping.

The Final Product: High Performance, Zero Waste

Our grown packaging is designed to replace Expanded Polystyrene (Styrofoam) and other plastic foams. It delivers comparable protective performance while being completely regenerative:

Premium Shock Absorption

The dense network of mycelium fibers provides high impact resistance, protecting heavy products and delicate electronics alike during rough transit.

Thermal Insulation

Excellent thermal properties make it suitable for cold-chain packaging, keeping goods temperature-controlled without the need for plastic coolers.

Hydrophobic & Breathable

Naturally sheds water while allowing humidity to escape, reducing condensation and keeping your products dry.

Fire-Resistant

The high chitin content in mushroom cell walls makes the packaging naturally flame retardant and highly self-extinguishing.

The Ultimate Lifecycle: Circular & Compostable

When the product reaches the end of its journey, disposal is simple. Instead of clogging landfills or polluting oceans for centuries, the final packaging is fully home-compostable. When broken up and put into backyard compost or buried in soil, it decomposes entirely within 45 to 60 days, returning nutrients back to the earth.
